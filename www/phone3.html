Connecting to Device

Task 3: Contacts
Part 1: Navigating to Contacts
The Contact Page on the Phone application contains a list of all the contacts saved on your phone. To access the Contacts page, place your finger on the home button, then move the finger straight up. Once you hear "Keypad", tap the screen twice.
Part 2: The Contacts Page
Displayed on the Contacts Page is a list of all contacts by name. Clicking on a contact will prompt VoiceOver to read out his details.
Part 3: Adding Contacts via Keypad (I)
One way of adding contacts is via the Keypad on the Phone Application. First, key in the phone number of the contact you wish to add. Then, touch the "Add Contacts" button located above number one on the keypad. Try finding the "Add Contacts" button below!
Part 4: Adding Contacts via Keypad (II)
Once you hear "Add Contacts", tap the screen twice. You will be asked whether you want to create a new contact or add to existing contacts. Select "create a new contact" and double tap the screen if you wish to add a new contact. This will redirect you to a new page.
Part 5: Adding Contacts via Keypad (III)
On the new page, the focus will automatically be shifted to the First Name textbox. It may be convenient to fill in the contacts' entire name into to avoid complications. Flicking left and right will direct you to other textboxes. Note that while flicking, VoiceOver will say "Is Editing" if the box you flicked to is the one you are currently typing in.
Part 6: Adding Contacts via Keypad (IV)
To type content in other boxes, touch or flick those boxes and double tap the screen, which will open the keyboard. Flicking further down will lead you to the Number section. The number dialled on the Keypad previously will be automatically saved as a Home Number by default. If this is incorrect, tap to the left of the number, then double tap. A list of labels will then appear. Flick to the appropriate label (like Mobile Number) and then double tap to change the label.
Part 7: Adding Contacts via Keypad (V)
Once you have finished keying in particulars, find the “Done” button on the top right corner of the screen, or by flicking right until VoiceOver reads "Done". Then, double tap the screen to save the particulars.
